Implementation Details

  • The worm is drawn using 3 ellipsoids having masses with springs between them. An orange torus ring is drawn as the worm’s scales so as to enhance the worm’s look.
  • The scene consists of a grass field having various garden objects and the worm’s house along with a path leading to the house.

Algorithm

  • For implementing the motion of a worm , a 2 spring - 3 mass system was implemented. The masses are implemented as ellipsoids(A,B,C) whose position and velocity vectors are calculated along the X,Y,Z directions .But the motion is 1-Dimensional along the X direction while the Y and Z coordinates for velocity and position are kept constant . The force acting on the masses was calculated using the formula: f = k*(L - l) - D*dl/dt
    where f = Force along spring direction,
    k = Spring force constant,
    D = Damping force,
    l = Current length of the spring,
    L = Minimum energy spring length
  • The velocity and position vectors of each mass are updated based on the acceleration acting on the particle. The acceleration was computed using the formula a = f/m. Based on acceleration value, the updated values of position and velocity vectors are:
    Velocity(new) = Velocity(old) + a*dt
    Position(new) = Position(old) + Velocity(new)*dt
    dt was maintained as 0.5 milliseconds for simulating the motion of the worm.
  • Effect of directional friction on translation : Due to the force acting on mass A the spring between A and B expands because of which there will be backward friction on A which results in mass A remaining stationary while B moves forward. Now the spring between B and C expands resulting in a backward friction on B making B stationary while C moves in forward direction. Due to this friction there will be translation along X axis of the spring mass - system.
    Directional friction is calculated by using a unit vector known as Spine Vector (S). This vector gives the direction of motion of the worm. It is computed using the formula:
    S = (XC-XA)/(|XA-XC|)
    The velocity of the individual particles is set as:
    If S*V < 0.0 then V = V - S(S*V)
    The above expression prevents backward sliding of the worm. Since motion of the worm is defined only along X axis:
    V = V - S(S*V) = 0; If S*V < 0.0

Issues and Problems Faced

  • There is a possibility of collision between the worm and the surface on which it moves . Collision detection and avoidance was not implemented.
  • Motion of worm on uneven surfaces and free fall motion includes the effect of gravitational force on the worm and is quite complex to implement.Hence to keep the implementation simple, the effect of gravity is ignored .
  • Experimentation was done with different refresh rates for glutTimerFunc. We used value of 50 as it allowed us to observe changes in the worm at a rate we considered optimal and pleasing to the eye.
